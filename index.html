What does this mean?

This means that they understand and appreciate that the land we are on right now is the original land of the Indigenous Peoples of Manitoba. They are commited to working with the Indigenous Peoples to understand the past wrongs done to them by Canada. Then, they explain that our water that we drink comes from Shol Lake 40 First Nations which is a Indigenous reserve. They express their thanks to the other First Nations Reserves for other things they give us.



What is a Treaty?

A treaty is an agreement or promise between two countries or nations and usually written and signed by all nations involved. Whatever promises that are included in the treaty signed by the leader need to be kept.

What nations or groups were a part of Treaty One?

Some communities that are a part of treaty one are: Peguis First Nation, Sagkeeng First Nation, Brokenhead Ojibway Nation, Roseau River Anishinaabe First Nation, Long Plain First Nation, Sandy Bay Ojibway First Nation, and Swan Lake First Nation, and the crown which is basically the Canadian high authority such as Parlement.
